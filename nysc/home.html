Registerable Works

The Nigerian Copyright Commission registers creative works across various categories. Here's a quick guide to some eligible works:

01

Literary Works

Includes books, articles, poems, reports, computer programs, and more—protected as long as they are original and fixed in a tangible medium.

02

Musical Works

Songs, compositions, lyrics, and sheet music—whether recorded or written—are all eligible for copyright registration.

03

Artistic Works

Paintings, drawings, photographs, sculptures, architectural works, and other visual art pieces can be registered under this category.

04

Audiovisual Works

Movies, documentaries, animations, and video content either independently produced or commercial are protected under copyright law.

05

Sound Recordings

Includes musical performances, podcasts, and other recorded audio content fixed on a medium and eligible for copyright protection.

Procedure for e-Registration

To register your creative work with the Nigerian Copyright Commission, follow the steps below via the NCeRS portal at www.eregistration.copyright.gov.ng.

  1. Click on “Copyright Registration”.
  2. Click on “Create an Account”.
  3. Fill out the account form with a valid email and submit.
  4. Check your email for login details and use them for future sign-ins.
  5. Log in and click “Register a New Work” on your dashboard.
  6. Complete the 11 sections of the form, clicking “Save and Next” to proceed.
  7. You may save and return later. Fields marked with * are required.

Quick tips for each section are provided on the right-hand side of the form page. Upon completion, you can save and print the form as a PDF.

Still Have Questions?

Our team at the Nigerian Copyright Commission (NCC) is here to support you. Whether you're an author, musician, filmmaker, software developer, or visual artist — we're ready to help you protect your intellectual property.

Email Support Learn More Call Us

How do I register my work with the NCC?

You can register your work through our e-Registration portal at eregistration.copyright.gov.ng. Simply create an account, fill in the required details, upload your work, and submit for review.

What types of works are eligible for copyright?

Copyright covers literary works, musical works, artistic works, cinematographic films, sound recordings, broadcasts, and computer programs. If it is original and fixed in a tangible form, it is likely eligible.

How long does copyright protection last?

Copyright protection generally lasts for the lifetime of the author plus 70 years after death. For corporate works, it is typically 70 years from the date of first publication.

Do I need to register to have copyright?

No, copyright is automatic once an original work is created and fixed in a tangible form. However, registration with NCC provides legal evidence of ownership and strengthens your rights in court.
